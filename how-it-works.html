WHATS THE THEORY?

How CrossyBot Works

Markets tend to move in trends — some quick, some lasting.
CrossyBot watches moving averages in real-time and acts when the signals show up.

What is a Moving Average?

A moving average (MA) is calculated using the average price over a specific time period then used to filter out noise and highlight the direction and strength of trends in price data.

ℹ️ Moving averages are foundational tools in technical analysis. They help traders:
• Identify trend direction
• Spot potential reversals
• & Identify dynamic support and resistance levels

A good beginner's video explaining moving averages is here.

What is an EMA Crossover?

An Exponential Moving Average (EMA) is a type of moving average which gives more weight to recent price data, making it more responsive to current price action.

ℹ️ A crossover occurs when:
1) A short-term EMA crosses above a long-term EMA (bullish)
2) Or when a long-term EMA crosses above a short-term EMA (bearish).

Either scenario signals a potential trend reversal or momentum shift.

Crossover strategies are time-tested tools for trend-following. They're simple, effective, and adaptive across almost all market conditions. By focusing on moving average relationships and what that means for the wider trend they help remove noise and reduce emotional decision-making.

How CrossyBot Uses Crossovers?

Crosses for Trade Opening

CrossyBot first identifies the larger trend using a higher timeframe EMA pair (from 1Day to 5min).
This filters trades based on the prevailing direction, reducing false signals during choppy or range-bound markets.

Once a trend bias is established, a lower timeframe (from 4Hour to 1min) EMA crossover is used to time entries.
This allows for sharper execution while still aligning with the broader trend.
Using dual timeframes aligns trade entries with higher probability setups reducing whipsaws and improving conviction.

ℹ️ Trend from higher timeframe = directional bias
ℹ️ Trigger from lower timeframe = signal to enter

Example:
1Hour chart shows 50 EMA > 200 EMA (bullish bias)
5min chart triggers 10 EMA crossing above 20 EMA → Enter spot trade

Crosses for Trade Closing

On a lower timeframe, when EMAs cross against the open trade direction, CrossyBot checks if the trade is in profit.

If it is, the trade closes automatically. If not, it stays open until closed manually or a stop loss has been hit.
